# Seoul — Discount Cards (Discover Seoul Pass)

**Purpose of this file:** list the discount cards available in Seoul, map them against the attractions *we actually visit* (Aug 11–18), calculate whether each pays off, and decide **which card, for whom, and for how long** to buy.

> **TL;DR** — The **Discover Seoul Pass 48h** is a **clear win for the 3 adults** (≈ +33,000 KRW each) *if* they do COEX Aquarium on Aug 18, and marginal (+5,000) if not. It is **break-even-to-negative for the kids** (there is no child discount), so **don't buy it for the 9- and 11-year-olds** — buy them discounted individual Lotte World + N Seoul Tower tickets instead. The 15-year-old is a coin-flip: buy only if COEX is confirmed.
> **Buy: 48h pass × 3 adults (activate afternoon of Aug 16). Kids: à la carte.**

## Cards available

The **Discover Seoul Pass** is sold **only to foreign passport holders** and has **one flat price for everyone aged 3–99 — there is no child/youth discount.** This single fact drives the whole recommendation below.

| Card | Duration / structure | Price (all ages) | Notes |
|------|----------------------|------------------|-------|
| Discover Seoul Pass **24h** | rolling 24h from first attraction scan | ~49,000 KRW | Being phased out on the official app; still on Klook/KKday |
| Discover Seoul Pass **48h** | rolling 48h | **70,000 KRW** | ⭐ the relevant one for us |
| Discover Seoul Pass **72h** | rolling 72h | 90,000 KRW | Timing-safety option |
| Discover Seoul Pass **120h** | rolling 120h | 130,000 KRW | Overkill for our attraction spread |
| **Pick 3 Basic** | choose any 3 attractions, 5-day validity, app-only | 49,000 KRW | ❌ theme parks (Lotte World) not eligible |
| **Pick 3 Theme Park** | 1 theme park + 1 of Group A/B + 1 of Group C, 5-day | 70,000 KRW | Group rule blocks Lotte World + N Tower + COEX together |

**Formats:** *Mobile* (QR in the app; bundles a free 5-day unlimited-data eSIM) or *Physical card* (doubles as a rechargeable T-money transit card; no eSIM). Pick-3 variants are mobile-only.

## Profitability (per person)

The pass earns its keep on three items that cluster **Aug 16→18**: N Seoul Tower + Lotte World + COEX. Everything else is either free anyway (palaces, museums) or *not* covered (Seoul Sky, cable car, Nami zipline).

**48h pass (70,000), activated afternoon of Aug 16 → expires ~Aug 18 afternoon:**

| Traveller | Value captured (N Tower + Lotte + COEX) | − pass | Verdict |
|-----------|------------------------------------------|--------|---------|
| Adult | 16,000 + 59,000 + 28,000 = **103,000** | −70,000 | **+33,000** ✅ |
| Child (9, 11) | 12,000 + 47,000 + 25,000 = **84,000** | −70,000 | +14,000 (thin) |
| Teen (15)¹ | ~16,000 + ~52,000 + ~27,000 = **~95,000** | −70,000 | ~+25,000 |

**If COEX is skipped** (it's only "Also Nearby"), just N Tower + Lotte World:

| Traveller | Value | − pass | Verdict |
|-----------|-------|--------|---------|
| Adult | 16,000 + 59,000 = **75,000** | −70,000 | **+5,000** (barely) |
| Child (9, 11) | 12,000 + 47,000 = **59,000** | −70,000 | **−11,000** ❌ loss |
| Teen (15) | ~16,000 + ~52,000 = **~68,000** | −70,000 | ~−2,000 |

¹ Age-tier note: the pass price is flat, but *gate* prices differ. At **Lotte World** a 15-year-old pays the **teenager** rate (~52,000); at **N Seoul Tower** the "adult" rate starts at 13, so the 15-year-old pays adult (~16,000). 9- and 11-year-olds get the child rate everywhere.

² **N Seoul Tower's gate price is channel-dependent** — roughly ~16,000–18,000 online-discounted vs ~27,000 at the gate. The tables above use the conservative ~16,000; a higher real price only *increases* the adult surplus and *reduces* the kids' shortfall, so the direction of every recommendation holds.

**Why the kids lose:** with no child discount on the pass but child *gate* prices ~15–20% lower on average, a child clears break-even only on the two priciest items together (Lotte World + COEX = 72,000) or all three — any combo leaning on the cheap N Seoul Tower falls short. The moment COEX is uncertain (as it is), the child pass goes underwater.

## Recommendation — what & how long to buy

- **3 adults → buy the 48h pass (70,000), activate the afternoon of Aug 16.** Then commit to the chain **N Seoul Tower (Aug 16 eve) → Lotte World (Aug 17) → COEX Aquarium (Aug 18 morning, before it expires).** Net ≈ **+33,000 each**.
  - If you can't reliably fit **COEX** before expiry, either step up to **72h (90,000)** for timing safety (net ≈ +13,000), or **skip the pass** and buy discounted individual Lotte World + N Seoul Tower tickets on Klook/KKday (Lotte World is routinely 10–45% off — that beats a barely-positive pass).
- **15-year-old → buy the 48h only if COEX is confirmed** (then ~+25,000). If COEX is doubtful, skip and go à la carte.
- **9- & 11-year-olds → do NOT buy the pass by default.** Buy discounted **child** Lotte World + N Seoul Tower tickets individually. Only buy the pass if you're certain they'll also do COEX (then +14,000), and accept that missing any one item flips it negative.
- **Duration verdict:** **48h is the sweet spot** — a 24h pass can't span N Tower (16th) *and* Lotte World (17th). 72h only buys timing insurance for COEX. The Pick-3 Theme Park (70,000) removes clock stress but its group rule blocks COEX and it's negative for kids — not recommended here.

**Concrete shopping list:** 3 × adult 48h pass (+ optionally the 15-year-old). Kids 9 & 11: individual discounted tickets. Everyone still pays out of pocket for **Seoul Sky, the Namsan Cable Car, and the Nami zipline** — none are pass items.

## Additional info & gotchas

- **Clock starts at your first *attraction* scan** — not at purchase, not calendar-day. **Sources disagree on whether issuing the free-AREX code also starts the clock**, so treat the AREX benefit as clock-starting: don't redeem it unless you mean to activate the pass. *Moot for us* — we arrive Aug 11 but don't activate until Aug 16, so we won't use the pass's AREX ride anyway.
- **No daily cap** on free attractions (time-passes) — visit as many as the clock allows, **once each**.
- **Lotte World = full-day admission but NOT the Magic Pass** ride line-skip. Grab the free same-day "Magic Day Pass" at the in-park stand (first ~100 guests) or just queue.
- **Voucher shelf-life** ~5 years before activation; refundable within 30 days if unused, non-refundable once activated.
- **Where to buy:** official app/site (discoverseoulpass.com — now foregrounds 72h/120h + Pick-3) or **Klook / KKday / Trazy / Pelago** (still stock the 24h/48h). No meaningful price difference between channels; the mobile version includes the eSIM.
- **⚠️ Re-verify before Aug 2026:** whether the **48h** is still sold, exact KRW prices (especially child gate prices), and Seoul Sky's discount status.
